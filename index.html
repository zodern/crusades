Crusades

Beginning

Muslims were advancing into the Byzantine territory. Byzantine emperor Alexius asked Pope Urban II for help against the muslims.

The goal was to free Jerusalem, Palestine and other holy places from the Muslims

Fun Fact

Everyone who participated in the crusades were forgiven of all their sins.

First Crusade
1095 - 1099

Pope Urban II launched the first crusade at the Council of Clermont in 1095.

They captured Jerusalem in 1099. The crusaders massacred many people when entering, even though they said they wouldn't.

4 crusader states were created to protect the land they gained. The states were Jerusalem, Edessa, Antioch, and Tripoli.

Second Crusade
1147 - 1149

The crusader state Edessa was captured in 1144 by the Turks. It was decided they needed a second crusade to send military reinforcements.

Was lead by Conard III of Germany and Louis VII of France

They attempted to capture Damascus. Nur al-Din, a Muslim leader arrived to defend Damascus. The crusade ended when the French and German armies were defeated by Nur al-Din.

Third Crusade
1189 - 1192

In 1187 Saladin, a Muslim commander, recaptured Jerusalem. Pope Gregory VII called for a crusade.

Led by the 3 most important leaders in Europe: Richard I from England, Philip II from France, and Federeick I from the Holy Roman Emperor.

In September 1191 Richard I defeated Saladin's army in the battle of Arsuf. It was the only real battle in the third crusade

Ended when Richard I and the Muslim leader Saladin compromised and allowed Christians access to the holy places.

Fun Fact

Every member of the army wore a cross.

Fourth Crusade
1202 - 1204

The new pope, Pope Innocent III, wanted another crusade. He called for one in 1198. The original plan was to conquer Jerusalem.

Went to Venice to pick up provisions for the trip (boats, horses, etc). The crusaders couldn't pay with oney so instead they agreed to help Venice capture the Byzantine port Zara.

Met Alexius IV in Venice and he convinced the crusaders to help him overthrow the the current ruler of Constantinople, Alexius III, and put him on the throne instead.

The crusaders declared war on Byzantine and looted the capital.

They put Alexius IV as the ruler of Byzantine.

Alexius IV was killed in 1204.

Fifth Crusade
1213 - 1221

Pope Innocent III called for the crusade, but died before it was carried out. Pope Honorius III toook over.

Most people were reluctant to join this crusade because of teh failure of the previus crusade.

Their goal was to capture Jerusalem by first capturing the Egyptian state of Ayyubid.

The crusaders marched through Egypt and captured the city of Damietta. They were defeated at Cairo when the crusaders failed to take in account the flooding of the Nile and was trapped behind it. They were forced to retreat while being attacked by the Egyptian army.

Sixth Crusade
years

The sixth crusade is the first crusade not initiated bythe authority or office of the Pope. Frederick II initiated it instead on his own because he was excommunicated (kicked out of the church).

When they arrived at the Holy Lands, they realized their army was much smaller than the army of the 5th Crusade, which had failed. He lied about his army size to the leader of the Egyptian forces Al-Kamil.

He succeed through diplomacy to delver Jerusalem, Nazareth and Bethlehem back to the crusaders and a 10 year long truce with the Egyptians.

Seventh Crusade
1248 - 1254

The Holy Lands had been taken over by the Turks in 1244.

The crusade was lead by Louis IX of France. His army had 35,000 ment.

Their goal was to capture key cities in Egypt. He thought they wouldn't be able to capture and keep Jerusalem if Egypt remained a hostile country.

They lost a quarter of their troops on the trip to Egypt.

They captured Damietta and al-Mansurah, but they lost many troops.

They decided to retreat back to Damietta, but the Egyptian armies ambushed and captured them.

800,000 bezants were paid in ransom for King Louis IX.
